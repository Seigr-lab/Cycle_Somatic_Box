# Step 17: Assemble Complete Electrodes

## Objective

Combine coated electrode quarters (from Steps 15-16) with central hubs and wiring to create 24 functional circular electrodes ready for installation in the 2m³ high-output system.

**Scale for high-output system:** This step assembles all 24 electrodes (12 cathodes + 12 anodes) with proper electrical connections and structural integrity for long-term operation.

## Why This Matters

**Electrode assembly determines system reliability:**
- **Structural integrity** - Must hold 2-4L biochar per electrode without collapse
- **Electrical continuity** - All quarters must connect to central wire (no open circuits)
- **Water/gas flow** - Gaps between quarters allow circulation (critical for microbial life)
- **Durability** - Assemblies endure 7-10 years in acidic, moist compost
- **Serviceability** - Individual quarters can be replaced if damaged

**Poor assembly causes:**
- Electrode collapse (biochar spills, shorts occur)
- Open circuits (dead zones, lost power)
- Blocked circulation (anaerobic pockets, low performance)
- Early failure (1-2 years instead of 7-10)

**For 24-electrode high-output system:**
- Each failed electrode = 5-10W power loss
- 3 failures × 8W = 24W total loss (12% of 200W target!)
- Proper assembly the first time prevents expensive rework

## Electrode Architecture

### **Complete Electrode Components:**

**1. Central hub (1 per electrode):**
- Circular disc: 80-100mm diameter
- Thickness: 15-20mm
- Function: Structural center, wire anchor point
- Material: 3D printed, laser-cut plywood, or handcrafted wood

**2. Quarters (4 per electrode):**
- 90° arc segments
- From Steps 15-16: Graphite-coated, cathodes have Mn
- Connect to hub via tabs/slots or fasteners

**3. Collection wire (1 per electrode):**
- Copper stranded: 18-20 AWG
- Length: 30cm from electrode center to connection point
- Function: Collects electrons from graphite coating → series/parallel array

**4. Fasteners (4-8 per electrode):**
- Screws, bolts, or zip ties
- Stainless steel or plastic (corrosion-resistant)
- Connect quarters to hub, secure wire

**5. Gasket/seal (optional):**
- Thin rubber, silicone, or fabric strips
- Placed at quarter-to-hub joint
- Prevents fine biochar leakage

### **Assembled Electrode Specifications:**

**Dimensions:**
- Diameter: 300mm (all 24 electrodes standardized)
- Thickness: 15-20mm
- Biochar capacity: 2-4L per electrode
- Weight empty: 200-400g
- Weight loaded: 800-1400g (with biochar + moisture)

**Electrical:**
- Resistance hub-to-quarter: <5Ω (good joints)
- Resistance across electrode: <20Ω (quarter to quarter)
- Wire gauge: 18 AWG handles 10A (safe for MFC currents <2A per electrode)

**Structural:**
- Load capacity: 5kg (2× safety factor for loaded weight)
- Lifespan: 7-10 years in compost environment
- Serviceability: Quarters removable for cleaning/replacement

## Assembly Process

### **Phase 1: Prepare Components (30-45 minutes for 24 electrodes)**

**1. Organize workspace:**
- Clean table or workbench
- Sort quarters: Cathodes (Mn-coated) separate from anodes
- Sort by electrode: 4 quarters + 1 hub per group (24 groups)
- Label groups: "E1" through "E24" (E = Electrode)

**2. Verify quarter labeling:**
- Each quarter marked with electrode number and position
- Example: "E5Q1" = Electrode 5, Quarter 1
- If not labeled, mark now (permanent marker on back)

**3. Test hub fit (dry assembly):**
- For each electrode group:
- Fit 4 quarters around hub without fasteners
- Check for gaps, misalignment, tight spots
- Note any quarters needing adjustment
- Sand/file tabs or slots if too tight

**4. Pre-drill hub holes (if needed):**
- If using screws or bolts and hub doesn't have holes:
- Mark 4-8 positions around hub perimeter
- Drill pilot holes (prevent splitting)
- Size: 3-4mm for #6 screws, 4.5mm for M4 bolts

**5. Prepare wires:**
- Cut 24 wires to 30cm length
- Strip 2cm insulation from both ends
- Tin one end with solder (pre-coating for easier connection)
- Label wire with electrode number ("E1" through "E24")

### **Phase 2: Electrical Connection (1-1.5 hours per batch of 6 electrodes)**

**Work in batches:** Assemble 6 electrodes at a time for efficiency

**6. Attach wire to hub:**

**Method A: Solder to embedded contact (if hub has metal insert):**
- Apply flux to hub contact point
- Heat with soldering iron (3-5 seconds)
- Touch tinned wire end to hot contact
- Feed solder until good fillet forms
- Remove iron, hold wire still 5 seconds (solder solidifies)
- Test: Gentle tug (wire shouldn't pull free)

**Method B: Mechanical crimp (if hub has wire channel):**
- Thread tinned wire through hub channel
- Fold wire back on itself (creates strain relief)
- Crimp with pliers or small bolt/nut
- Test: Pull with 2-3kg force (should hold)

**Method C: Wrap and solder (universal method):**
- Drill small hole through hub center
- Thread wire through, wrap around 2-3 times
- Solder wrap to itself (creates solid loop)
- Pull wire tight, ensure good contact with graphite coating on quarters

**7. Create hub-to-quarter electrical connection:**

**Connection point:** Where quarter meets hub
- Hub should have graphite coating OR conductive pad
- Quarter has graphite coating extending to hub contact area
- Wire connects both through hub

**Ensure continuity:**
- Before assembly, test with multimeter:
- Probe 1: Touch wire
- Probe 2: Touch quarter graphite coating (at hub edge)
- Target: <5Ω resistance
- If >20Ω: Add graphite paste bridge (wire → hub → quarter)

**8. Apply conductive paste at joints (recommended):**
- Mix small amount of graphite paste (from Step 15)
- Dab paste at hub-quarter interface points
- Ensures electrical contact even if mechanical fit isn't perfect
- Let tack 30 minutes before final assembly

### **Phase 3: Mechanical Assembly (30-45 minutes per batch of 6 electrodes)**

**9. Assemble first quarter:**
- Position Quarter 1 against hub (align tabs/slots)
- Ensure wire isn't pinched
- Insert fastener (screw, bolt, or zip tie)
- Tighten partially (allow adjustment)
- Check: Quarter sits flush against hub, no gaps >2mm

**10. Add remaining quarters (2, 3, 4):**
- Work around hub in order (Q1 → Q2 → Q3 → Q4)
- Each quarter should:
  - Touch hub securely
  - Align with adjacent quarters (90° arc each)
  - Leave small gap between quarters (1-3mm) for water/gas flow
- Insert fasteners, tighten partially

**11. Adjust and align:**
- View electrode from above (should be circular)
- Check gaps between quarters (1-3mm is ideal)
  - Too tight (<1mm): File edges slightly
  - Too wide (>5mm): Reposition or add shim
- Check electrode is flat (not bowed or twisted)
  - If bowed: Adjust fastener tightness

**12. Final tightening:**
- Once alignment is good, tighten all fasteners firmly
- For screws: Snug (don't overtighten - can crack graphite coating)
- For bolts: Use wrench, tighten until lockwasher compresses
- For zip ties: Pull tight, trim excess tail

**13. Seal joints (optional but recommended):**
- Apply thin bead of silicone caulk around hub perimeter
- Fills micro-gaps (prevents fine biochar leakage)
- Wipe excess with damp cloth
- Cure 12-24 hours before loading biochar

### **Phase 4: Quality Control (10-15 minutes per electrode)**

**14. Structural test:**
- **Visual inspection:** Electrode is circular, flat, no visible gaps >3mm
- **Shake test:** Hold by wire, shake gently - quarters shouldn't rattle or move
- **Flex test:** Apply gentle pressure to opposite edges - minimal flexing
- **Fastener check:** All screws/bolts tight, none missing

**15. Electrical continuity test:**

**Setup:** Multimeter in resistance (Ω) mode

**Test 1: Hub to each quarter**
- Probe 1: Touch wire at electrode
- Probe 2: Touch graphite coating on Quarter 1
- Record resistance: Should be <10Ω
- Repeat for Quarters 2, 3, 4
- **If any >20Ω:** Poor connection, disassemble that quarter, add graphite paste, reassemble

**Test 2: Quarter to adjacent quarter**
- Probe 1: Quarter 1 graphite coating
- Probe 2: Quarter 2 graphite coating
- Should be <15Ω (path through hub)
- Repeat: Q2-Q3, Q3-Q4, Q4-Q1
- **If >30Ω:** Hub connection problem, check wire solder joint

**Test 3: Across entire electrode**
- Probe 1: Quarter 1 edge
- Probe 2: Quarter 3 edge (opposite side)
- Should be <20Ω
- This tests full electrical path across electrode

**16. Water drainage test:**
- Pour 200ml water slowly onto assembled (empty) electrode
- Water should drain through gaps between quarters
- Drain time: <30 seconds for 200ml
- **If water pools:** Gaps too small or sealed shut, need to open slightly

**17. Label and document:**
- Mark electrode clearly on non-coated surface:
  - "CATHODE #X" (for cathodes 1-12)
  - "ANODE #X" (for anodes 13-24)
  - Mn-enhanced status (cathodes only)
  - Assembly date
- Record in notebook:
  - Electrode number
  - Resistance measurements
  - Any issues or repairs
  - Pass/fail status

### **Phase 5: Storage Before Installation (Until Step 18)**

**18. Protect assembled electrodes:**
- Stack carefully with separators (cardboard, foam) between layers
- Keep dry (moisture can cause premature biochar swelling)
- Store wires extended (don't coil tightly - can stress connections)
- Cover with cloth (protect from dust)

**Storage organization:**
- **Container 1:** Cathodes (electrodes 1-12)
- **Container 2:** Anodes (electrodes 13-24)
- Separate storage prevents mixing during installation

## Troubleshooting

**"Quarter won't fit hub (too tight)"**
- Sand or file tabs/slots slightly
- Check for graphite coating buildup (scrape if too thick)
- Hub may have dimensional variation (measure with calipers)

**"Gap between quarters is too wide (>5mm)"**
- Hub too small or quarters too short
- Add shim material at hub (thin plastic, wood veneer)
- Or accept wider gap (still functional up to 10mm)

**"Electrode wobbles or isn't flat"**
- Hub is warped (common with 3D prints)
- Tighten fasteners in star pattern (opposite sides)
- Place on flat surface while tightening
- If severe: Replace hub

**"Electrical resistance is high (>20Ω hub to quarter)"**
- Wire connection to hub is poor (resolder)
- Graphite coating doesn't extend to hub contact area (add paste bridge)
- Oxide on wire (clean with sandpaper, re-tin with solder)
- Hub isn't conductive (needs graphite coating or metal insert)

**"Fasteners strip or break during tightening"**
- Pilot holes too small (drill larger)
- Overtightening (use light touch)
- Material too brittle (3D print layer adhesion failure)
- Switch to bolts with nuts (more forgiving)

**"Biochar will leak through gaps between quarters"**
- Small gaps (1-3mm) won't leak biochar (particles are 2-10mm)
- If worried: Apply thin bead silicone sealant
- Or line electrode with fabric disc before loading (Step 18)

**"Wire connection fails (pulls free from hub)"**
- Solder joint too cold (didn't flow properly) - reheat and add solder
- Mechanical stress on wire (add strain relief loop)
- Hub material pulled out (use bolt-through method instead)

**"Can't tell which electrodes are cathodes vs anodes"**
- Visual: Cathodes have brown Mn fragments visible, anodes are gray-black
- Label: Mark clearly before assembly (permanent marker on back)
- Separate storage: Different containers prevent mixing

## Advanced: Modular Sub-Assembly Strategy

For easier handling and testing, consider modular approach:

**Sub-assembly 1: Hub + Wire (pre-assembled):**
- Attach wire to all 24 hubs first
- Test electrical connection
- Store until ready for quarter attachment

**Sub-assembly 2: Quarter Pairs:**
- Temporarily connect 2 quarters with single fastener (forms half-circle)
- Easier to handle, less delicate than individual quarters
- Attach pairs to hub later (2 pairs + hub = complete electrode)

**Advantages:**
- Easier testing (can test hub wiring before final assembly)
- Better quality control (catch problems early)
- More portable (sub-assemblies easier to transport)

**Trade-offs:**
- More handling steps
- Need temporary fasteners
- Slightly longer total time
